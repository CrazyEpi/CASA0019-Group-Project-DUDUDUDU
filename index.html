NoiseGuard

DATASET
This Data Device uses real-time sound data collected from a microphone sensor, including loudness (dB) and pitch (Hz). The system also classifies the detected pitch into musical notes (e.g., C, D#, F).

DATA DEVICE (Physical)
 The physical device visualizes sound loudness through a servo-controlled gauge that moves a pointer across green–yellow–red zones. In order to maintain a better learning environment and effectively reduce the noise in the learning area the dial is designed according to the "School Chapter" of the World Health Organization's "Guidelines for Community Noise". The green area represents the "Compliance Zone", with a sound level of 0 - 35 dB, which meets the standards, providing 100% speech clarity, no additional cognitive load on the brain, and is suitable for high-level learning. The yellow area is the "Interference/Compensation Zone", with a sound level of 35 - 50 dB, which is sub-optimal acoustically, with a damaged signal-to-noise ratio. Students need to use "executive functions" to filter out the noise,resulting in fatigue and memory decline. The red area is the "Disruption & Health Risk Zone", with a volume of 50 dB or above, causing information masking, loss of most speech information, and accompanied by the secretion of stress hormones, essentially interrupting the learning process. Loudness in dB is shown using dual 7-segment displays, while pitch and musical note are displayed on the LCD panel. This multi-layer physical feedback allows users to intuitively understand sound intensity and tone characteristics in real time.

DATA DEVICE (Digital)
 The digital twin of the data equipment provides additional information. It consists of two main parts. The first part is the model of the physical equipment. In this model, the pointer on the dial will rotate as the volume changes, and below it will display the exact volume value. Moreover, the color of the physical model shell will change from purple to blue and finally to yellow as the surrounding sound frequency increases. The second part is a dashboard with four charts, including two ringcharts and two linecharts. The ringcharts show the exact volume and frequency, while the linecharts display the volume and frequency of the past five samples.

HOW TO
 Open the app and scan the dial of the physical device, and the AR digital twin will be displayed on the phone.
